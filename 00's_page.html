History of The Internet

00's

Event 1: 2000 Aaron Swartz Co-Creates RSS Aaron Swartz co-creates RSS, a program that collects news from various web pages and puts them in one place for readers, with the goal of making information freely available to everyone.

Event 2: 2001 Aaron Swartz Helps Build Creative Commons Under the leadership of Harvard Law School Professor Lawrence Lessig, Aaron Swartz helped build the open architecture for Creative Commons, which works to minimize the barriers to sharing and reusing research and educational materials.

Event 3: 2001 Jimmy Wales Launches Wikipedia Jimmy Wales launches Wikipedia. There are half a million Internet users.

Event 4: 2006 Nancy Hafkin Pens "Cinderella or Cyberella?" Dr. Nancy Hafkin authors Cinderella or Cyberella?: Empowering Women in the Knowledge Society, a collection of essays that examines how information and communications technologies empower women.

Event 5: 2007 Apple Releases It's First iPhone Priced at $499 for 4GB and $599 for 8G.
